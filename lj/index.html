Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills...

October 27, 2021

There are pictures which hold a story in them and it is important to teach close reading and visual thinking skills to understand this story better.

One of the ways to be detective. Careful attention to detail rewards the viewer with a “big picture” understanding. Notice the details!

The best way to learn something is to do it regularly. Practice regularly on explaining to yourself “What’s Going On?” this picture, to help you practice close reading.

Research other ideas and opinions. What do you think about observations made by anyone else? Answering this question will help you evaluate and link to other ideas.

After asking yourself “What’s Going On?” this picture start asking more detail oriented and try to prove your conclusion by finding more evidence.

When many questions rise the answers should appear too. This is called conversation and it is important while learning close reading. Start conversations regarding particular photographs.

After discussing about the photograph learn the real story that stands behind this work. Ask photographers about what they were thinking when they took the shot.

There are many other ways of learning visual literacy. Feel free to study them all.

How to Avoid Misusing Lightboxes...

October 20, 2021

Overlays helps to quickly bring new information without losing the contents of the current screen. The most popular intensions of an overlay is to view content, complete a form, or make a decision.

The most important rules when creating overlays are: provide visible “close”; make it distinct from background; make the overlay keyboard friendly; avoid scrolling and links inside the overlay.

To make a perfect overlay, designer has to answer 5 questions:

  • Who is the audience? Consider the possible resolutions of all users devices. A large overlay on mobile can cause a need to zoom out which disorients the user.
  • What actions users has to take? Designer should make it simple and quick for the user to process the information on an overlay. This will become possible after designer will exactly see what users have to do on the page. For example, if a user will have to complete a form in the overlay, the designer should add a cancel button and save the inputs in case of accidental canceling.
  • When an overlay will appear? Be careful with the overlays that are not user-initiated. It can really annoy them. Make those in the right time. For example, when the user leaves.
  • Where it will appear? Try to position your overlay nearer to the top of the page.
  • Why it is not a page? Prefer an overlay instead of a page only if the user has to take an action with serious consequences, it’s needed to collect a small amount of info, the content in the overlay is urgent.

Plan your overlays appropriately.

Exploring the best practices for form design...

October 17, 2021

First impressions last forever! This is an important principle for a modern web-site which has a mission to convert. One of the most important components of a successful design are forms. Here I described the best practices for form design.

The amount of input fields does not have to overwhelm the user: try to separate into series of input fields and do not forget to display the progress. Do not expect that the user will enter a valid input: it is a good practice to indicate the requirement for a valid input right away. Keep the labels close to the input field and determine the best position based on the screen size. Very first input field should be active and highlighted so the user do not make a redundant click. In addition, test your input fields for their “Tab” key friendliness. Different buttons should differ and have enough space between each other. Their label has to continue the sentence “I want to…”. People like to know their results, so always return a feedback even if the form is submitted successfully. Remember, “Reset” button will only make it worse…

Lately, I fill out a lot of forms by applying for jobs. Usually the forms are long and overwhelming, but some of them are really nicely designed forms. One of these job applications is Facebook application form. One of the best parts is that the amount of input fields is less than other companies have. I also can track my progress looking at the left progress bar. I like that they use autocomplete input fields because it helps me to find a value quickly and I do not have to type it all. There are so many other good design ideas implemented which you can check by applying on your own here.
